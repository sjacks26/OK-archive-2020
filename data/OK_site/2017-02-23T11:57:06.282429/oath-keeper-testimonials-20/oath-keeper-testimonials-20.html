Gregg O’Neill:

Testimonial: Hi, this is Greg O’Neill, I served with honor for nine years of active duty, with a hitch in the Navy (70-74), serving in the Tonkin Gulf, building bombs on the attack aircraft carrier, KITTY HAWK (CVA-63), and later in the Army (79-83) after a drunken bosun’s mate chief recruiter took off on leave, putting my record jacket in his locked drawer, which caused me to miss my window of opportunity to get back in the Navy, so I joined the Army. Back in 70 – 74 they did not have PTSD diagnosed, so we faced being pariahs when we went home from the “War” as they call it now. I dealt with guilt for building bombs to kill innocent folk in Vietnam, Cambodia and Laos, when we were ordered to be silent about what we were doing, in violation of international agreements, bombing countries we didn’t declare war on, including Vietnam, North and South.

I am not ashamed of taking the oath to serve our country, to defend our constitution against enemies, foreign and domestic, but now I’m disgusted to see those enemies in DC, and in the Pentagon, who serve corporate interests over the needs of our country, and states, they have forgotten who they are serving. Our young people are in Iraq and Afghanistan, not to defend our nation, but to serve the OIL industry, and the drug runners making money from their growing poppies in Afghanistan, and making heroin from selling it to junkies here in the States. There is a company in England now making synthetic fuels, identical to those refined from crude oil, extracting hydrogen and carbon from the air to make hydrocarbons, why aren’t we using that instead of serving Big OIL?

When I attended OCS Class 1-80 at Fort Benning we were told to watch the  senior officers coming out of command classes at Infantry Hall, for them to serve as our role models, what a bunch of slackers, out of shape duffel bags, fat boys, promoted with over inflated efficiency reports from their brown nosing commanding officers. How many of them have made money from the corporations in the military industrial complex that make weapons systems, and supplies, for our armed forces?

We know what the problems are, now what do we do about it? I, and my friends, have access to advanced technologies to provide every state with abundant clean energy, food and water, we are up against a wall put there by those who would rather kill us than to see these technologies used to put our country back to work, and on our feet again to stand tall, and proud of our nation, self-reliant, and independent as we once were.

I’d love to see other vets and active duty personnel ending the “wars” in other countries, and getting to work here to turn our country around, and put back on track to being a country governed by our constitutional law, free of tyranny from DC, and the corrupt feds. Tell the slackers at the Pentagon to do what is right, and to do what they know needs doing NOW.

Back in the 1970’s me and my father, John B. McMillan, were reading Mother Earth News and saw the section about, The Economic Outlook. So off to back to the land world we went. We bought a beautiful 30 acre farm that was in the family at one time in Barrow, County, Georgia. After several years of bringing the old home place back upscale we had a wonderful place. It was a old dairy farm with lots of outbuilding, Lakes, pastures and woodlots.

So, to keep a long story short, this standoff in Oregon has some of us remembering the damage done to families when confronted with the task of standing up for our rights as Americans. That brought the Law Enforcement to the door… and this was Sept. 30th, 1982, his birthday. A SWAT team from Athens Georgia came on the farm and shot him 18 times for Terrorist Threats. They blocked off all the roads in the area and I was the first one of the family to arrive on the scene since it was his birthday. So, we got Bobby Lee Cook to represent us in a Civil Rights Violation…since it’s Martin Luther King day civil stories work. So now at 64, disabled but still upright I’m wondering if this Oregon action is effecting other people of the US Patriot Community. Now I’m looking for a battle rifle, AR, some camo and some people to hang out with. Both my grown children who lived on the farm are disabled now. I hope the checks keep coming…but don’t let them say disabled people are exempt of the 2nd Amendment.

Blessings, Tom McMillan , Whittier, NC
