New master version of Jordan Page’s “Arm Yourselves” will debut on Thanksgiving Day

Oath Keepers is proud to announce the upcoming release of “Arm Yourselves,” a no-holds-barred anthem for the 2nd amendment written by our friend and honorary OK member, Jordan Page.

On Thanksgiving Day 11/26/15, the link for Jordan’s recording of his song “Arm Yourselves” will be available to download on all major retail sites including CDBaby, iTunes, Napster,  Amazon, Google Play Store, and many more.

Please share this link with your friends and families to download the song — www.cdbaby.com/jordanpage6.  (Note: That link is not active until Thanksgiving day, November 26, 2015.)

Many of our members contributed to the crowdfunding campaign this fall to raise funds to get the song recorded, and the finished product is more than we hoped for.  Oath Keepers will proudly use this song for outreach campaigns to our active troops and much more.  With participation from CATI Armor, Tactical Supply, Roberts and Roberts Precious Metals Brokerage, and more forthcoming sponsors, Jordan will be filming a new music video for these songs to make the case for liberty and the 2nd amendment. The video will feature members of Oath Keepers, so keep an eye out for updates on this.  Remember to download Arm Yourselves and its companion piece, “Act III”, on Thanksgiving Day.

Visit Jordan Page‘s website for more information: www.jordanpagemusic.com

From Jordan’s GoFundMe page, this paragraph:

Jordan Page has been on the forefront of the national stage since 2008 championing freedom causes and speaking out against war, government corruption, and the rise of the police state in America.   From opening for Congressman Ron Paul at dozens of events on the 2012 presidential campaign trail for thousands of people, to national television and radio appearances, to headlining at hundreds of protests and liberty outreach events, Jordan has developed a national reputation for speaking truth to power through music and is a premier voice of liberty in America.
