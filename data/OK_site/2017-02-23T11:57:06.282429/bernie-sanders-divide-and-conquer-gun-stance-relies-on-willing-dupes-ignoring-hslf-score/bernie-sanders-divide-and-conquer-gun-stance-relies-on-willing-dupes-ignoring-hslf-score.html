Bernie Sanders’ Divide-and-Conquer Gun Stance Relies on Willing Dupes Ignoring Backers

0 0 0 4
by David Codrea , January 27, 2016

The only thing we need to realize about Bernie Sanders and guns is that he understands Chairman Mao’s observation that “Every Communist must grasp the truth; ‘Political power grows out of the barrel of a gun.'” (Campaign flyer scan by @gdebenedetti)

“We need to make sure that certain types of guns used to kill people, exclusively, not for hunting, they should not be sold in the United States of America,” openly socialist presidential wannabe Bernie Sanders told NBC’s “Meet the Press,” InfoWars reported. “Coming from a rural state, I think I can communicate with folks coming from urban states where guns mean different things than they do in Vermont where it’s used for hunting.”

It’s a divide-and-conquer strategy familiar to gun rights advocates who have seen it time and again, notably illustrated by with the excommunication and partial redemption of outdoor writer Jim Zumbo some years back, after he called semiautomatics “terrorist rifles.” And while we see no shortage of politicians trying to con us out of our firearms by telling us what we do or don’t need to shoot deer with, the unalterable fact remains, the Second Amendment ain’t about hunting.

It’s natural that Sanders would pander to the hunting crowd though, coming from Vermont and all. That’s because he had to. But now that he’s on the national stage, if he ends up winning, the concerns of jeopardizing his home state’s three electoral votes won’t weigh as heavily on his decisions. Besides, it’s not like anyone dumb enough to vote for Bernie is going to change their mind if he betrays his hunter constituency – realistically, his stock will go up.

So what of the doctrinaire “Fudd,” who cares only about duck season and wabbit season, and doesn’t care what else Bernie bans? “Do not ask for whom the bell tolls” and “I shall eat you last” come to mind.

All you need to do is look at Bernie’s campaign website positions on “animal welfare”:

Bernie Sanders is an advocate of animal welfare and ensuring animals’ humane treatment. He has co-sponsored several pieces of legislation to protect the interests of animals and has recently received a 100 percent rating from the Humane Society Legislative Fund (HSLF).

And who are they?

…a social welfare organization incorporated … formed in 2004 as a separate lobbying affiliate of The Humane Society of the United States. HSLF works to pass animal protection laws at the state and federal levels, to educate the public about animal protection issues and to support humane candidates for office.

And where does the Humane Society stand on all those hunters Bernie is out pandering to?

The HSUS is strongly opposed to the hunting of any living creature for fun, trophy, or for sport…

Basically, authorized wildlife management officials or Inuit subsistence hunters get a pass. Does anyone think the Humane Society is going to give its highest mark to a candidate who works against its interests?

And basically, Bernie Sanders is just another lying Bolshevik, relying on useful idiots until such time as he no longer needs them.

Isn’t that a bit strong?

Sanders-cofounded the Congressional Progressive Caucus, an affiliate of the Democrat Socialists of America. Maybe Bernie would like to flesh out how he intends to take “non-sporting” arms away from those of us who own guns for purposes other than hunting, and what he thinks will happen if he tries. Especially since, as author L. Neil Smith of The Libertarian Enterprise notes:

Bernie Sanders is the advocate for a political system that murdered over two hundred million people in the twentieth century alone.

If you don’t mind — and especially if you do — I’ll keep my “guns that kill.”
