Red-Green Axis Spells Out Danger Threatening to Destroy the Republic

0 0 0 0
by David Codrea , August 24, 2015

The whole point behind consent of the governed is, ultimately, “to secure the Blessings of Liberty to ourselves and our Posterity.” That’s not what those who are betraying their country are doing.

I just finished “The Red-Green Axis: Refugees, Immigration and the Agenda to Erase America.” by investigative reporter James Simpson. The monograph, published by the Center for Security Policy, warns how the intentional “fundamental transformation of America” with Third World immigrants and refugees – many of whom are “resettled” unvetted and later proven to harbor terrorist intentions —  is a form of assisted national suicide—one that we the people are forced by our “leaders” to pay for.

It’s a short read, easily doable within a couple hours. That’s why it surprises me to struggle with what to emphasize in this overview – no doubt because Simpson covers so much ground and packs it so densely with information necessary to the survival of the Republic. So naturally, most Americans will remain oblivious to its existence, and if word is to get out, it’s up to those of us who heed his warnings.

But instead of me, let him explain it. Watch this brief video, where author Simpson gives us an overview of his work and the danger it exposes.

Simpson analyzes the various subversive agencies and foundations striving to “consume the host’ with “seedlings.” That amplifies an analogy I made earlier this month, comparing the threat to the 50s cult classic “Invasion of the Body Snatchers,” in which our population is targeted for replacement by alien invaders. He then ties in familiar subversive names like George Soros, and demonstrates how everything unfolding falls into place with United Nations objectives. He shows us the culpability of co-opted churches that advance Marxist dogma and totalitarian goals, as opposed to American exceptionalism and traditional values. It’s curious, how the “progressives” cheering this on never seem to bring up “wall of separation” objections  (incidentally– you will want to check if your church is helping with the destruction, and respond accordingly).

I can say, without hyperbole, this is a vital report addressing nothing less than the survival of the Founder’s Republic in the 21st Century, one that you ignore at your peril, and at the peril of everyone you love. I cannot urge you in strong enough terms to get it and read it, and to do that without delay. Like now.
