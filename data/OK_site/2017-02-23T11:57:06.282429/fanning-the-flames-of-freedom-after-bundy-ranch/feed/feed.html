In about 4 months during the winter of 2013-2014, the people — mostly the generation born in the 1990s — toppled the Yanukovych government. The president was a convicted felon, he refused to honor his election promises, the parliment refused to act and the police beat and shot prosters who pleaded with them to honor their oaths to protect the Ukraine rather than killing fellow citiizens and defending a corrupt government. The raw courage of the Ukrainian people — and the numbers involved — is remarkable in that they faced down internal security and police without weapons. Mostly burning tires, wearing football and motorcycle helmets (that the Ukrainian parliment subsequently outlawed), throwing rocks and protecting themselves with tin shields and sticks. The Ukrainian revolution, like the Arab Spring movements, was fueled by social media communication platforms.

Makes one appreciate what governments will do to remain in power and the wisdom of the 2nd Amendment. I hope that, unlike their Ukrainian counterparts, our military and police — on orders from higher ups — would not open fire on fellow citizens, but would honor their oaths to defend the Constitution.

Today, we live in a world where people are increasingly dissatisfied with the incumbent power structures — e.g., the top 3 Republicans in the Presidental race have never served in policial office and are riding on a wave of discontent with the state quo. Whatever else you think of them, the Black Lives Matter movement is politically the other side of dissatisfaction with the current political power structure. The Middle East has a long string of governments that were recently taken out by the people they were supposed to be serving, and wer’e living with the chaos of the failed state in Syria. This is, indeed, the Chinese curse of “May you live in interesting times.”
