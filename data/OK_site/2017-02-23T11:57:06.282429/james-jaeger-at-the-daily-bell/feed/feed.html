Thank you for reading here and leaving your comments. In response to issues you have brought up I would like to add just a bit of clarification for all readers.
First, the article above is an interview with James Jaeger by Anthony Wile of The Daily Bell. It is not an official Oath Keepers article, although James Jaeger promotes the Oath Keepers mission with his latest film, Midnight Ride. James Jaeger was speaking on behalf of his own studio, Matrix Entertainment, and he was not speaking as a policy-maker for Oath Keepers. That’s one thing that should be noted please.

The author of the book which inspired James to make Midnight Ride is Edwin Vieira. Edwin is a member in the NRA and is also an NRA-certified firearm instructor. He joins James in lamenting the fact that the NRA has refused to acknowledge, for many years, the first thirteen words in the 2nd Amendment. Both Edwin and James, and I myself as an associate producer on the film, wanted the NRA to have a representation in the movie, but we could not get any interest from the NRA. Ted Nugent is a lifetime member in Oath Keepers, and is a member of the Board of Directors for NRA, and we tried repeatedly to interest him in representing the NRA in the movie, to no avail. So James is lamenting the fact that the NRA shows no real interest in the complete meaning of the Second Amendment, or at least not enough interest in it to have a presence for NRA in this movie. That’s a second note we all should bear in mind.
But thirdly, please understand that not one of us who were involved in making the documentary film Midnight Ride is “attacking” the NRA. We are questioning the NRA’s motive for avoiding the word “Militia”, when the Constitution itself requires it and the 2nd Amendment to that Constitution also says the Militia is “necessary to the security of a free State”. When the duty of the Federal government is clearly spelled out in the Constitution and the Bill of Rights, (the duty to see that the “several States” have their Constitutional Militia), and a gun-rights organization refuses year after year to acknowledge publicly that the Constitution requires the States to have their Militia, what does that say about the gun-rights organization? It is not an attack on the NRA for refusing to support the Constitution, but instead, James, and Edwin, and actually quite a number of others including myself, are seeking to guide the leadership of the NRA in their thinking, so that they will see the light and do the right thing — which is to remind this berserk government about what the highest law of the land has to say about the government’s duty to make sure the States have their Militia. The NRA should, and could, be educating millions of loyal NRA members about the Militia, yet it remains silent. I for one am glad the NRA is there, but I see no reason not to urge the NRA to consider the whole Second Amendment, instead of just focusing on the last part of that Amendment. I don’t see that as “attacking” the NRA, but as offering grassroots (and Constitutional) guidance.

If, as you noted, “the NRA obviously knows what they are doing….”, then that is an indictment, a confession that they are deliberately refusing to acknowledge what the highest law of the land requires. I prefer to think that they just have not looked deeply enough into the “Original Intent” of the Founders and into the spirit of the written Constitution and Declaration of Independence, and that they should be willing to admit truth when loyal members bring it to them in undebatable presentations such as the works of Edwin Vieira and the documentaries of James Jaeger.

Thank you for reading.
Salute!
Elias Alias, editor
